Aromagraphy Performance: Gluttony
Authors: Kristiāna Šuksta (poet) and Sandris Murens (aromagrapher)

Gluttony is an experimental performance exploring meat consumption through the combination of poetry and scent. By combining language, olfaction, and time, Gluttony invites the audience to construct personal meanings and reflect on desire, memory, and the sensory dimensions of meat consumption. The performance develops the language of aromagraphy by demonstrating how sequences of text and scent create new emotional and conceptual layers of meaning.

Built from twelve words, each paired with its own distinct aroma, the work continuously varies the sequence, tempo, and pauses between reading and smelling. Through both congruent and incongruent relationships between language and scent, the performance reveals how olfactory experience can reinforce, contradict, or transform the interpretation of a text, producing multiple possible readings and sensory narratives.

By constantly reshuffling the order and timing of words and scents, the performance disrupts linear chronology and creates a form of shuffled time, where meaning emerges through ever-changing temporal configurations rather than a fixed sequence. Each iteration reorders memory and anticipation, allowing past associations and present sensations to coexist in new and unpredictable ways.
