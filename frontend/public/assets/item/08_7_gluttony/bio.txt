Sandris Murins is a Latvian artist, inventor, and researcher working at the intersection of scent, technology, and contemporary art. He is the inventor of the Scent Camera, an experimental device designed to capture, store, and reproduce odors, expanding the possibilities of sensory documentation beyond photography and sound recording.

As the founder and director of the Scent + Art Festival, Murins actively promotes aromagraphy as an emerging artistic discipline in which scent functions as a key medium of multisensoriality for storytelling, memory, and spatial experience. His multisensory performances and installations combine smell with the body, text, sound, and visual elements to provoke reflection on perception, identity, and contemporary culture, challenging audiences to experience art through embodied and often overlooked senses.

Kristiāna Šuksta

Kristiāna Šuksta is a Latvian poet, performance artist, and technical producer of the performance festival Starptelpa. Her artistic practice combines poetry and live performance to create self-reflective works that explore personal experience, everyday life, and human relationships through wit and humor.

Working across literature and performance, Šuksta embraces experimentation and audience engagement, often using playful language and performative situations to reveal deeper emotional and social layers. Her work balances introspection with irony, inviting viewers to reflect on themselves while maintaining a sense of lightness, curiosity, and critical awareness.
