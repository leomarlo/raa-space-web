The performance unfolds inside a room that changes over time. At the beginning, the space contains
a body, a set of objects, and a series of event-score cards: short, open-ended instructions that invite
actions such as cutting, tying, spilling, recording, producing sound, and so on. Audience members
draw and perform these scores, gradually transforming the room through gestures, traces, sounds,
relations, and disruptions. As the work progresses, the same kinds of actions return, but never
under the same conditions. An instruction such as “cut” or “spill” shifts from pure potentiality
into an action whose meaning depends on the relations already formed in space and time: on what
has been moved, connected, broken, recorded, or left behind. Each action inherits the consequences
of previous actions while opening new possibilities for what can happen next. Inspired by Fluxus
1
event scores, the piece treats the room as a metaphor for social and historical time: a space shaped
by accumulation, instability, memory, and adjustment. Through the interaction between performer,
audience, objects, and traces, the work explores how new meaning emerges in changing conditions.
